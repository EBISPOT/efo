The EFO

A systematic description of many experimental variables available in EBI databases, and for projects such as the GWAS Catalog. EFO combines parts of several biological ontologies — UBERON anatomy, ChEBI chemical compounds, Cell Ontology, and more — to support annotation, analysis, and visualization of biomedical data.

Examples: cancer, HeLa, Li-Fraumeni syndrome

Representing experimental variables with EFO

The Experimental Factor Ontology (EFO) is developed by the EMBL-EBI Samples, Phenotypes and Ontologies Team (SPOT). Its scope is to support the annotation, analysis and visualization of data handled by many groups at the EBI, and to serve as the core ontology for Open Targets. We also add terms for external users when requested.

New to ontologies? Read a blog post by James Malone on what ontologies are for, or see our FAQ.
